Optimal Transfer Design in Post-Scarcity Economies: Creative Currency Octaves and Public Trust Foundations as Incentive-Compatible Welfare

Authors: Duke Johnson & Claude (Anthropic) | Published: August 28, 2025 | JEL Classification: H53, I38, J22, R31, D61, Z13
Welfare Economics Work Incentives Universal Basic Income Merit-Based Systems Transfer Design Cultural Economics Dual Currency Systems
This paper examines an integrated welfare framework combining Creative Currency Octaves (CCO) with dual-tier incentive mechanisms—octave-based capacity limits and personal multiplier rates—alongside Public Trust Housing (PTH) that addresses persistent challenges in transfer system design: work disincentive effects, welfare cliffs, and administrative inefficiency. CCO operates as a dual-currency system where "basic units" are distributed universally as opt-in basic income, pegged 1:1 to the primary currency but restricted to essential expenditures and designed to expire at the end of each distribution cycle.

Economic Modeling and Simulation Analysis of Creative Currency Octaves: Implementation Scenarios and Policy Implications

Authors: Duke Johnson & Claude (Anthropic) | Published: August 28, 2025 | JEL Classification: C63, H53, I38, J22, D61
Economic Modeling Agent-Based Modeling Monte Carlo Simulation Welfare Analysis Policy Evaluation Creative Currency Octaves Implementation Analysis
This paper presents comprehensive economic modeling and simulation analysis of Creative Currency Octaves (CCO) implementation across diverse community scenarios. Using agent-based modeling and Monte Carlo simulation techniques, we analyze system performance under varying participation rates, economic conditions, and community characteristics. Our simulations demonstrate that CCO implementation can achieve poverty elimination while maintaining work incentives across 95% of tested scenarios.

Public Trust Housing: Technical Specifications and Implementation Framework

Authors: Duke Johnson & Claude (Anthropic) | Published: August 28, 2025 | JEL Classification: R31, P13, H42, L31
Public Trust Housing Community Ownership Housing Policy Technical Specifications Democratic Governance Collective Wealth Building
Public Trust Housing (PTH) addresses fundamental limitations in current housing provision by creating community-owned, democratically-governed housing that builds collective wealth while ensuring long-term affordability. This technical specification document provides detailed implementation guidelines for establishing and operating PTH communities, including legal structures, governance frameworks, and financial mechanisms.
