Optimal Transfer Design in Post-Scarcity Economies

👥 Duke Johnson & Claude (Anthropic) 📅 Published: August 2025 📄 45 pages 🔓 CC BY 4.0 License

Abstract

This paper presents an integrated welfare framework combining Creative Currency Octaves (CCO) with dual-tier incentive mechanisms that addresses persistent challenges in transfer system design. Unlike traditional welfare systems that create marginal tax rates exceeding 50% at benefit phase-out ranges, the enhanced CCO-PTH framework eliminates benefit reduction entirely while maintaining strong merit-based incentives through both volumetric capacity expansion (octave levels that double conversion capacity) and qualitative conversion multipliers (1x to 14x+ rates based on contribution quality).

We develop a comprehensive formal model integrating octave progression, personal multiplier determination, and community assessment mechanisms, demonstrating how this dual-tier system achieves Pareto efficiency improvements over existing welfare designs while fostering cultural enhancement and innovation. Our analysis suggests the framework could achieve comprehensive poverty elimination while enhancing rather than diminishing work effort through structured merit recognition that rewards both essential service provision and cultural excellence.

Keywords: Creative Currency Octaves, welfare economics, transfer design, incentive compatibility, post-scarcity economics, dual-tier incentives, poverty elimination

1. Introduction

The fundamental challenge in welfare system design has long been balancing adequate support for those in need with maintaining incentives for productive contribution. Traditional means-tested programs create implicit marginal tax rates often exceeding 50% as benefits phase out, creating "poverty traps" that discourage work and advancement.

This paper introduces a revolutionary approach through the Creative Currency Octaves (CCO) framework integrated with Public Trust Housing (PTH) systems. By replacing benefit reduction with capacity expansion and quality multipliers, we eliminate the equity-efficiency tradeoff that has plagued welfare economics for decades.

2. The Dual-Tier Incentive Structure

2.1 Volumetric Capacity (Octave Levels)

The octave system provides eight levels of conversion capacity, with each level doubling the monthly conversion limit:

  • Level 0 (Base): 1,000 X-cents/month
  • Level 1: 2,000 X-cents/month
  • Level 2: 4,000 X-cents/month
  • Level 3: 8,000 X-cents/month
  • Level 4: 16,000 X-cents/month
  • Level 5: 32,000 X-cents/month
  • Level 6: 64,000 X-cents/month
  • Level 7: 128,000 X-cents/month

2.2 Qualitative Multipliers

Personal multiplier rates range from 1x to 14x+ based on productivity, creativity, and community impact assessments. This creates strong incentives for quality contribution while maintaining universal basic support.

3. Mathematical Framework

The formal model demonstrates how the dual-tier system achieves optimal transfer design. Let W(e,θ) represent welfare as a function of effort (e) and type (θ), where:

W(e,θ) = U(c(e,θ)) - ψ(e,θ)
c(e,θ) = y(e,θ) + T(e,θ) × M(e,θ)
where T represents octave capacity and M represents multiplier rate

This structure ensures ∂W/∂e > 0 for all e, eliminating work disincentives while maintaining progressive support distribution.
